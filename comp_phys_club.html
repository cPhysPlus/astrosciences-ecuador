Who we are?

The Computational Physics Club is focused on providing students with a structured environment to explore and apply computational methods in physics, with a specific focus on Python and Mathematica. Our goal is to equip students with the skills needed to tackle complex physical problems through numerical simulations, symbolic computation, and data analysis.

Our Work

At the Computational Physics Club, we explore the intersection of physics and computational methods to tackle complex problems in science and engineering. Our members engage in projects spanning numerical simulations, data analysis, and algorithm development using Python and Mathematica.

What We Do:

  • Numerical Simulations: We model physical systems, from classical mechanics to quantum phenomena, leveraging computational tools to solve equations that are otherwise intractable.
  • Data Science & Machine Learning: We apply statistical methods and AI to analyze experimental data, classify patterns, and enhance scientific predictions.
  • Interdisciplinary Projects: Our work extends beyond physics, collaborating with engineers, mathematicians, and computer scientists to solve real-world challenges.
  • Hackathons & Competitions: We organize coding challenges, including our annual hackathon, to foster innovation and sharpen problem-solving skills.
  • Workshops & Research: Through biweekly meetings, we host workshops on scientific computing and contribute to open-source projects in computational physics.
